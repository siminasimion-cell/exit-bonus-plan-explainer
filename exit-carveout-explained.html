Your Upside in a Change in Control

We want you to share in the success you help create. Here's how the Change in Control Bonus Plan works — and what it could mean for you.

Overview
How the Bonus Plan Works

If our investors (the "Sponsor Group") sell a significant portion of their ownership — what we call a Change in Control — a bonus pool is created and shared among eligible employees. The better the exit, the larger the pool.

📈

Exit Multiple Matters

The bonus pool scales with the Sponsor's return on investment (MOI). At 1.5x MOI, the pool is 1% of net proceeds. At 2x it's 1.5%, and at 3x it reaches 2%.

🎯

Your Tier Sets the Baseline

Every employee is assigned a Tier (1, 2, or 3) based on their role. Higher tiers receive a larger unit allocation — and you can move up by earning promotions.

⭐

Performance Multiplies Your Share

Your actual payout is weighted by your performance and impact rating (1–5). Strong and exceptional performers earn significantly more units.

Your Position
Understanding the Tiers

Your tier is based on your role at the time of a transaction — not when you receive your letter. That means promotions between now and a Change in Control directly increase your potential payout.

Eligibility
What You Need to Know

To be eligible for the bonus, you must remain in continued service and good standing with the company from the date of your letter through a Change in Control event. Your unit allocation is determined by three factors: your tier (role level), your tenure, and your performance and impact.

The Bonus Plan is subject to Board approval. The specific legal terms — including exact definitions of "Change in Control" and how Sponsor MOI thresholds are calculated — will be communicated separately once the Board approves the plan.

FAQ
Common Questions
What is a Change in Control?
A Change in Control occurs when the Company's investors sell a significant portion of their ownership in the Company. Think of it as an exit event — a sale, merger, or similar transaction where the sponsor investors realize a return on their investment.
What is Sponsor MOI?
Sponsor MOI stands for "Multiple on Investment." It measures how much the sponsor equity investors earn relative to what they originally put in. For example, a 2x MOI means they doubled their money. The higher the MOI, the larger the bonus pool for employees.
Can my tier change before a transaction?
Yes! Your payout is based on your tier at the time of the transaction, not when you received your letter. If you earn a promotion that moves you to a higher tier, your potential payout increases accordingly.
What happens if I leave the company before a Change in Control?
You must remain in continued service and good standing from the date of your letter through the Change in Control event to be eligible. If you leave before then, you would not receive a payout under this plan.
How is my individual payout calculated?
The total bonus pool is divided into units allocated across all eligible employees. Your payout equals your units divided by the total units, multiplied by the total pool. Your unit count is influenced by your tier, tenure, and performance rating.
Are these payout numbers guaranteed?
No. The numbers shown in the calculator and table are illustrative estimates based on assumptions (175 employees, specific MOI levels). Actual payouts will depend on the final terms approved by the Board, the actual transaction economics, and the number of eligible participants at the time.
